Membership & Reports

Keep your plan in one place — without giving up control

Maisage starts as a free resource: plain-language guides, planning ideas, and tools you can print or adapt on your own. Membership and paid reports are optional layers for people who want more structure, storage, or help turning all the pieces into one clear plan.

The goal is simple: cover the cost of thoughtful work without turning your decisions into a sales funnel.

Money and transparency come together here

If Maisage ever offers paid options, they will follow the same rules as the free content:

  • No commissions, referral fees, or kickbacks from providers, plans, or products.
  • Clear, simple pricing — no surprise add-ons.
  • You keep ownership of your decisions. We help you see options, not choose for you.

Anything described on this page is about structure and intent. Actual pricing and availability may change over time and should always be confirmed on the most current version of this site.

Free layer – use the tools without paying a cent

The free layer is for anyone who wants to learn, plan, and compare options on their own, using public information and simple frameworks.

Always free access to:

  • Learn: plain-language guides on Medicare, Medicaid, hospice, and medicines.
  • Plan: frameworks for thinking about home, care paths, and long-term costs.
  • Scenarios: example paths like Medicaid spend-down, family support hybrids, and equity blends.
  • Tools: printable-style worksheets, checklists, and comparison tables.
  • Living Map: a method for building your own local view of options and costs.

You can use the free materials privately, with your family, or with professionals you already trust.

Membership – for people who want structure and tracking

A future membership option is intended for people who want a private place to save their inputs, track revisions, and get guided prompts — without turning their data into a product.

What membership is meant to include

  • Digital versions of planning tools with saved entries.
  • A simple dashboard to see key details in one view (income, supports, care paths).
  • History of changes, so you can see how your plan has evolved.
  • Guided questions based on what you’ve already entered — not random checklists.

Pricing philosophy (example)

  • Simple monthly range (for example, around $10–$15/month) if/when membership launches.
  • No long-term contracts or lock-ins — cancel whenever it stops being useful.
  • Clear explanation of what is and is not included before you pay anything.

Exact pricing and features may change. The constant is the commitment to neutrality and transparency.

Personalized “Family Aging Plan” report

Some families don’t want a subscription. They want a one-time, structured document they can print, share, and update on their own. A future option is a personalized “Family Aging Plan” PDF.

What this type of report could include

  • Summary of your current situation (in your own words, organized clearly).
  • Overview of likely care paths you’re considering (home, hybrid, facility-first, etc.).
  • Pros, limitations, and questions to ask for each path — tailored to your inputs.
  • Space for notes from family meetings and professional conversations.

A sample price point for this type of report might be in the range of around $75, depending on how much human review is included. The exact structure and pricing would be clearly listed whenever this becomes available.

The goal is to pay for the time and thinking involved — not to steer you toward any particular product, provider, or legal/financial decision.

Who membership and reports are for (and not for)

A good fit if:

  • You like seeing everything organized in one place.
  • You want to keep track of your own planning over months or years.
  • Your family needs a neutral framework to look at the same information.
  • You’re okay paying a modest amount for structure and clarity — not for products.

Not a good fit if:

  • You want someone else to take over decisions or manage finances for you.
  • You’re looking for investment products, insurance sales, or legal documents.
  • You expect real-time crisis case management or 24/7 on-call support.

Common questions

Does paying for membership change the advice I get?

No. Paying would only change the format (dashboards, storage, reports), not the values or neutrality. Maisage does not receive money from providers, plans, or products — only from you, if you choose to pay for extra structure.

Will my information be used for marketing?

The intent is to keep any collected information limited to your use and necessary service operation — not to sell or lease it to third parties. Details would be spelled out in any future privacy policy before you sign up.

Is this a replacement for professional advice?

No. Membership and reports are for clarity, organization, and navigation. They are not legal, financial, or medical advice and do not replace working with licensed professionals.

Want to start with the free tools first?

You don’t need a membership to begin planning. Use the Learn, Plan, Scenarios, Tools, and Living Map pages to get oriented. If you later decide you want more structure or a written plan, you can come back here.
