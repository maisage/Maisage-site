Private pay, savings, and family contributions

Many care arrangements are paid for from a mix of sources: monthly income, savings, proceeds from selling a home, and help from family members. Seeing how these pieces fit together can make discussions more concrete and less emotional.

This page does not tell you what you should do. It offers a framework for thinking and talking about who pays what, for how long, and under what conditions.

Common building blocks

  • Monthly income (Social Security, pensions, annuities).
  • Savings and investments.
  • Home equity (if a home is sold or borrowed against).
  • Family contributions, either ongoing or short-term.

Each family will weigh these pieces differently. What matters is that everyone understands the general picture and the tradeoffs.

Family help: making it visible

When family members contribute, it can help to:

  • Write down who is contributing what (money, time, housing, coordination).
  • Talk about how long each contribution is realistic.
  • Consider what happens if one person’s situation changes.

Clear expectations do not remove all stress, but they can reduce misunderstandings and hurt feelings later on.
