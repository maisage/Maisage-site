Start here: a simple snapshot of what’s going on

This page helps you organize the basics of your situation: who needs care, what’s happening, how urgent it feels, and what boundaries you’re working with. It isn’t about perfection — it’s about getting out of your head and onto one page.

1. About the person needing care

Capture enough detail that someone outside your family could understand the situation at a glance.

You don’t have to list every detail. Focus on what is making life hard, unsafe, or unsustainable right now.

2. What’s changing, and how fast?

This helps distinguish “we’re worried but stable” from “we feel like we’re drowning.”

3. Money, boundaries, and capacity

This is not about exact budgets. It’s about getting a rough sense of what might be realistic.

Go to tools and scenarios
Right now, this page does not automatically save or send data anywhere. You can use your browser’s print-to-PDF or copy/paste into your own notes, or share it with your care team.

How to use this page well

Don’t overthink it. A rough, honest snapshot is more useful than a perfect, delayed one.

  • Fill this out in one sitting, even if it’s messy.
  • Then walk away for a bit and come back with fresh eyes.
  • Share it with one trusted person and ask, “Does this match what you see?”
  • Bring it to appointments with doctors, care managers, or attorneys.

The goal is to give you language and structure when you’re tired and overwhelmed — not to judge how you’re handling things.

Where to go after this

Once you have a snapshot that feels roughly right, you can:

  • Use the Tools page to compare home vs. facility options, care mixes, and rough cost paths.
  • Use Facility Insights if moving or touring facilities is on the table.
  • Use Services if you want structured help turning this snapshot into a clearer, short-term plan.

You don’t have to solve everything at once. Even one clarified decision is progress.

If you’re in a true crisis (someone is unsafe, suicidal, severely short of breath, confused, or has a sudden change in condition), skip this page and contact emergency services or your medical team directly. This page is for planning, not emergencies.
