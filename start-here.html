What this page is for

This isn’t a legal form. It’s a way to make sure everyone is talking about the same situation: the same person, the same changes, the same time frame.

  • Who is at the center of decisions.
  • What’s changing in health, memory, or function.
  • What feels urgent in the next few months.
  • Where money, medications, or housing feel stuck.

After this, tools like stay vs. move comparison, scenario planning, or medication list tend to go much smoother.

1. Who is at the center of decisions?

Use names and relationships that make sense to you. There’s no “correct format.”

2. What is changing or not working right now?

Focus on concrete changes over the last 1–6 months.

3. What feels most urgent this month?

There might be many problems, but urgency is usually about the next few weeks.

4. Money, benefits, and stress points

Rough notes are enough. Exact numbers can come later.

5. Medications and medical complexity

Note if medications are part of why things feel hard or expensive.

If medications are a big part of the stress, the Tools → Medications section and the medication list tool may be a good next step.

6. Facilities or housing options you’re already considering

Write down anything that’s even “just an idea.”

If you already have a facility shortlist, the Facility Insights dashboard and facility questions checklist can help you decide where to look more closely.

7. Anything else we haven’t captured yet

Use this for dynamics, family disagreements, or anything that doesn’t fit neatly above.
