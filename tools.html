Tools to plan, compare, and prepare

These tools are meant to give you a clearer picture of what’s happening, what’s possible, and what to ask next — not to tell you what to do. Use as few or as many as you need, in any order.

Scenario planning tools

These tools help you compare realistic paths side by side — not fantasy versions. The goal is to see trade-offs in money, workload, and stress, not to find a perfect plan.

Stay vs. move comparison

Compare “stay at home,” “move to assisted living,” and one hybrid option (such as moving in with family plus outside help).

  • List monthly costs for each path.
  • Capture who does what (driving, meds, night checks, coordination).
  • Note likely stress points and what could break each plan.
Open stay vs. move tool →

Short-term vs. long-term view

Compare “stabilize for the next 3–6 months” with “what if we keep doing this for 1–2 years.”

  • See which plans are sustainable vs. just possible once.
  • Flag where you’d likely need to adjust or move again.
Open timeframe planning tool →

What Matters Most alignment

A short exercise to line up care decisions with the person’s values, preferences, and non-negotiables.

  • Clarify what they care about most day to day.
  • Check whether current options honor that or conflict with it.

Medications: cost & complexity tools

These tools do not change prescriptions. They help you see which medications may be driving cost and complexity and prepare better questions for prescribers and pharmacists.

Medication list & tiers

Map current medications, note who prescribed each one, and group them by importance and suspected cost.

  • List all prescriptions, over-the-counter drugs, and supplements.
  • Mark which ones seem essential, helpful, or “not sure.”
  • Note any you suspect are expensive or hard to manage.
Open medication list tool →

Pairs well with the Learn page on medication costs.

Pharmacy cost comparison helper

A simple structure for checking prices for key medications across pharmacies or discount programs.

  • Pick a small set of high-cost medications to compare.
  • Record quotes from different pharmacies or programs.
  • Prepare questions about formulary and alternatives.
Open cost comparison helper →

Questions for prescribers & pharmacists

A structured set of questions to bring to your next appointment or medication review.

  • Clarify purpose, benefits, and realistic expectations.
  • Ask about side effects that matter most in daily life.
  • Ask whether any medications could be simplified.
