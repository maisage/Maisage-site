Tools

Simple tools to make complex decisions easier to see

These tools are meant to help you organize what you already know, what you’re being told, and what you still need to find out. They don’t replace professional advice or official sources — they help you walk into those conversations more prepared.

Many of these can start as printed checklists or simple tables. Over time, some may become interactive calculators or dashboards — but the core idea stays the same: clear, transparent, and on your side.

How to use these tools

  • Use them to see the whole picture on one page — not scattered in emails and notes.
  • Bring them to family meetings, provider visits, or financial reviews.
  • Update them as things change instead of starting from scratch each time.

These tools are for planning and clarity. They are not legal, financial, or medical documents.

Planning & budget tools

These tools focus on helping you see how income, expenses, and care costs fit together over time. They don’t tell you what to choose — they help show what each choice might mean.

Monthly Life & Care Budget

A simple worksheet for listing income, fixed bills, flexible spending, and current or future care costs on one page.

  • Social Security, pension, and other income.
  • Housing, utilities, insurance, and medications.
  • Home help, transportation, and other support costs.

3–10 Year Planning Snapshot

A forward-looking view that asks: what if health, housing, or support needs change in 3, 5, or 10 years?

  • Current situation vs “if nothing changes.”
  • Early move vs late move into higher-support housing.
  • How long savings might last under different paths.

Care path comparison tools

These tools help you compare different care options side by side — home support, assisted living, memory care, or nursing facilities — so you can see tradeoffs more clearly.

Home vs Assisted Living Comparison

A table to compare staying at home with support versus moving into assisted living.

  • Monthly costs at home versus facility base rates.
  • What’s included vs extra at each option.
  • Impact on independence, social contact, and safety.

Scenario Comparison Sheet

A worksheet that mirrors the three main scenarios: Medicaid spend-down, family support hybrid, and reverse equity blend.

  • Summarizes assumptions for each path.
  • Notes likely timeline and main risks.
  • Links back to the Scenarios page.

Medication & health information tracking

These tools are about keeping information coordinated — so you don’t have to start from zero every time someone asks, “What medications are you on?” or “Who is your main doctor?”

Medication & Pharmacy List

A simple list you can keep updated and bring to appointments or use when checking medication prices.

  • Drug name, dose, and how/when it’s taken.
  • Prescribing doctor and primary pharmacy.
  • Notes for alternatives or generic options to ask about.

Health & Provider Snapshot

A one-page summary of key conditions, providers, and emergency information for quick reference.

  • Primary care and key specialists.
  • Major diagnoses and allergies.
  • Preferred hospital or system, if any.

Conversation & documentation helpers

These tools are about keeping people on the same page — you, your family, and your providers. They don’t replace formal documents, but they help you prepare for them.

Family Meeting Outline

A simple structure for conversations about support, expectations, and limits.

  • What you want to stay in control of.
  • What support you’re asking for — and what you’re not.
  • How decisions will be revisited as things change.

Questions for Professionals

A reusable list of questions for doctors, social workers, financial planners, and facility staff — with space to record answers.

  • “What does this not include that people assume it does?”
  • “What are common problems families run into with this option?”
  • “What would you recommend if this were your own parent or partner?”

Future interactive tools

Over time, some of these worksheets may be offered as interactive calculators or dashboards. Even then, the rules stay the same:

  • Clear assumptions.
  • Transparent logic — no black boxes.
  • Links to underlying public information where possible.

Maisage does not store or sell personal financial or medical data. Any future interactive tools will be designed to keep you in control of what you enter and what you share.
