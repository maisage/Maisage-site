Planning tools — you stay in control of decisions

Tools to see trade-offs before you commit

These tools are meant to help you frame decisions, compare paths, and spot questions to bring to your doctors, care team, and advisors. They don’t replace professional advice — they help you prepare for it.

Medication cost & complexity view

Medications are often a hidden driver of both cost and complexity. This section is built to help you map out what’s on the list, see where the cost drivers are, and identify questions to bring back to your prescribers and pharmacist.

  • List current medications, doses, and frequencies.
  • Flag meds that are typically higher-cost or need prior authorization.
  • Identify combinations that may warrant a pharmacist or prescriber review.
  • Capture questions like “Is there a generic?” or “Can this be safely deprescribed?”

The goal is not to tell you what to stop or change — it’s to help you have a more focused, productive conversation with the people who know your medical history.

In a future release, this area can be wired to external pricing tools and formularies. For now, it functions as a structured planning worksheet that you can download or bring to appointments.

How to use this with your care team

When you’ve filled out the medication view:

  • Share it with your primary care physician and any specialists.
  • Schedule a dedicated medication review visit or pharmacist consult.
  • Ask about cost, side effects, interactions, and deprescribing options.
  • Confirm before making any changes — never stop meds on your own.

Think of this tool as the “prep work” for a better, safer conversation about medications and budget.

Care scenario & financial tools

These tools help you compare “what if we do X vs. Y?” over the next 6–24 months, using rough planning numbers rather than precise forecasts.

Home vs. Assisted Living comparison

Outline a simple picture of what staying at home would require (hours of help, family time, equipment) versus moving to an assisted living or similar setting. Includes high-level cost ranges and non-financial trade-offs.

Care mix planner (family + paid help)

Sketch a realistic mix of family help, paid caregivers, adult day programs, and respite. Get a clearer sense of what’s sustainable for both the person needing care and the people providing it.

Simple cash-flow view for care

Map out current income, key fixed expenses, and estimated care costs to see whether a plan looks workable, tight, or unrealistic — before you sign anything binding.

“What are we forgetting?” checklist

A structured checklist that prompts you about transportation, nights/weekends coverage, backup plans, legal documents, and family dynamics that often get missed in the rush.

Facility research tools

These tools tie into the Facility Insights dashboard and help you turn patterns in the data into practical next steps.

Shortlist builder

Start from facilities that look promising on paper (or that you already have in mind) and apply filters based on distance, budget, care level, inspection history, and ownership stability.

Tour & call question generator

Turn facility patterns — recent deficiencies, staffing flags, ownership changes — into concrete, plain-language questions to ask on tours and phone calls.

Apples-to-apples comparison sheet

Compare 2–4 facilities on a single page: base rates, care level, staffing, inspection issues, “fine print” fees, and subjective notes from your family’s impressions.

Using these tools alongside real professionals

These tools are designed to make your conversations with doctors, pharmacists, care managers, attorneys, and financial planners sharper — not to replace those conversations.

When in doubt, share what you’ve built in Maisage with your care team and ask them where the plan looks realistic, where it’s optimistic, and what “red flags” they see. A modest, workable plan is better than a perfect but impossible one.
