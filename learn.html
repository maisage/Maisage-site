Medicare – where to look, what to watch

Medicare is the federal program that covers hospital and medical care for people over 65. It has limits — especially around long-term care and daily home support.

Where to go for official information:

  • Medicare.gov – Official site
  • What Medicare Covers
  • Costs & Premiums

What to pay attention to:

  • Medicare covers treatment, not long-term custodial care.
  • Advantage plans may limit networks and approvals.
  • Copays and deductibles can stack quickly.

Medicaid – eligibility, spend-down, estate recovery

Medicaid can help with long-term care once income and assets are low enough. Rules depend on your state.

Where to go:

  • Medicaid.gov – Federal overview
  • Your state’s Medicaid site (look up “<your state> Medicaid LTC eligibility”).

What to pay attention to:

  • Income and asset tests.
  • Home care vs facility coverage differences.
  • Estate recovery rules after death.

Maisage does not interpret state law — we highlight what to read carefully.

Hospice – what most people don’t know

Did you know?

Hospice care does not cover room and board. This surprises almost every family. Hospice pays for medications, equipment, and nursing visits — but not the place where you live.

If hospice is provided inside assisted living, memory care, or a nursing facility, those facility charges still have to be paid separately.

This is why planning matters — so room and board costs don’t become a late-stage crisis.

Official information: Medicare.gov – Hospice Coverage

Medicines – finding lower prices without guesses

Medication prices vary wildly between pharmacies and payment methods. Maisage does not sell medications — we show you how to compare.

Examples of tools to check:

  • Major pharmacies’ own price-check tools.
  • Non-insurance discount programs that list cash prices.
  • Manufacturer assistance program pages.

Questions to ask:

  • Is this price for brand or generic?
  • Cash, discount, or insurance price?
  • Is there another pharmacy offering a lower cash rate?

Maisage does not recommend specific pharmacies — only how to compare safely.

Care options & costs – grounding decisions in reality

Costs vary based on region, provider, and support needs. Instead of quoting numbers that change, Maisage shows you where to find current ranges and how to interpret them.

Where to look:

  • State aging agencies.
  • Regional cost-of-care surveys from non-profits.
  • Local facility and home-care websites.
