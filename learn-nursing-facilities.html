Nursing facilities and skilled care

Nursing facilities (often called nursing homes) provide a mix of medical care, rehabilitation services, and help with daily activities. Some stays are short-term for recovery; others are long-term when ongoing support is required.

Many facilities participate in Medicare and Medicaid, and they are subject to specific regulations and inspections. Public data about them is available, but it has limits.

Short-term rehab versus long-term placement

It can help to distinguish:

  • Short-term rehabilitation – often after a hospital stay, focused on recovery and returning home, sometimes covered by Medicare for limited periods when criteria are met.
  • Long-term custodial care – ongoing residence when someone needs daily help with many activities and cannot safely live elsewhere.

Different funding sources and rules can apply to each type of stay, even within the same building.

Understanding public ratings and reports

In the United States, facilities that participate in Medicare or Medicaid are typically listed on federal and state websites with:

  • Overall star ratings and domain-specific ratings (staffing, health inspections, quality measures).
  • Inspection reports and citations.
  • Ownership and sometimes staffing information.

These resources are useful, but they may lag real-time conditions and do not capture every aspect of daily life in a facility. They work best when combined with visits, conversations, and ongoing observation.
