Facility questions checklist

Use this as a worksheet for tours and calls with assisted living, memory care, or nursing facilities. You don’t have to ask every question — pick the ones that matter most in your situation.

Basics

  • What level of care is your facility licensed to provide?
  • What happens if care needs increase (for example, more help with transfers or dementia progression)?
  • Is there a waitlist, and how does it work?

Staffing and care

  • What is your typical staff-to-resident ratio during days, evenings, and nights?
  • How do you handle falls, sudden confusion, or medical changes?
  • Who coordinates with outside providers (primary care, specialists, home health, hospice)?

Routines and daily life

  • What does a typical day look like here?
  • How are meals, activities, and quiet time handled?
  • How do you accommodate personal preferences (wake times, bathing, religious practices, etc.)?

Costs and contracts

  • What is included in the base rate? What is billed as an add-on?
  • How often do rates increase, and how much notice do families receive?
  • Are there community fees, assessment fees, or other one-time charges?

Red flags and follow-up

Make notes on:

  • Anything that feels unclear, rushed, or minimized.
  • How staff respond to questions about past problems or inspections.
  • Whether what you see on the tour matches what you were told.

Tip: Print this page or save as PDF and bring it with you. Add your own questions based on your situation.

Important: This checklist does not recommend or endorse any facility. It is for planning and question-asking only.
