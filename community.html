Community & Volunteers

No one should have to navigate this alone

Maisage is built from lived experience — and it works best when that experience is shared. This page is about the people side: volunteers, navigators, family members, and professionals who care about transparency, independence, and dignity in aging.

There are no referral fees, commissions, or “preferred providers” here. The goal is to connect insight, not to steer you toward any product or facility.

What “community” means in Maisage

  • Families sharing what they’ve learned the hard way, so others don’t have to.
  • Volunteers and navigators helping people understand options, not sell solutions.
  • Professionals contributing insight without turning it into an advertisement.

This is not a social network or crisis hotline. It is a slow, steady effort to make real-world experience more visible and usable — without exploiting it.

Become a Maisage Navigator

A Maisage Navigator is someone who helps others understand the landscape — not someone who gives orders or sells services. Navigators might be adult children who’ve been through this once already, retired professionals, or people who simply care about clear information.

What Navigators might do

  • Help someone list their options in plain language.
  • Walk through Learn, Plan, and Scenarios pages with a family.
  • Point people to official sources for their state or situation.
  • Flag confusing language or hidden assumptions in materials.

What Navigators do not do

  • Give legal, financial, or medical advice.
  • Guarantee outcomes or promote specific providers.
  • Pressure anyone into a decision or product.
  • Collect or sell personal data.

If a Navigator program is formally launched, expectations, training, and boundaries will be clearly defined before anyone is matched with families.

Interested in becoming a Navigator?

For now, the simplest way to express interest is to use the Start Here page and mention that you’d like to help as a Navigator. Over time, this may become its own form.

Coverage by region (concept, not a directory)

The idea of a “coverage map” is simple: show where volunteer Navigators, lived experience, and local knowledge are available — not just where facilities are located.

Until there are enough Navigators to make a real map useful, this section stays conceptual. When it goes live, the principles will be:

  • No paid placement or “premium listing” for providers.
  • Clear labels for volunteers vs professionals.
  • No public display of personal contact info without consent.
  • Ability for families to search by region and type of help (guidance only, not services).

Any future map would be about visibility and connection, not ratings or rankings.

Stories and lessons from real families

Honest stories are often more useful than any brochure. Over time, this section can include anonymized or consented stories that highlight what went right, what was harder than expected, and what people wish they had known earlier.

Example: “We thought hospice covered everything”

A family discovers late that hospice does not cover room and board — and how that changed their decisions about where their loved one stayed and how long private funds lasted.

Example: “Three siblings, three expectations”

Siblings with different incomes and beliefs about inheritance use a structured conversation to agree on who can do what — and what happens if someone’s situation changes.

These are illustrative patterns, not actual case files. Any real stories shared in the future would require explicit consent and careful anonymization where appropriate.

Partners and acknowledgments

If Maisage ever collaborates with non-profits, academic groups, or community organizations, this is where those relationships will be named — along with exactly what they do and how money (if any) flows.

  • Clear listing of any organizations that help improve the content or tools.
  • Disclosure of any funding or grants, if they ever exist.
  • No “silent partners” whose influence is hidden from families.

Right now, this is an intention, not a list. The principle is that any partnership must support transparency and independence, not undermine them.
