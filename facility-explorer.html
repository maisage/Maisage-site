Facility Insights: using public data to see how places are really operating

This section explores how Maisage can use public information — such as CMS data, state inspection reports, and other official records — to give you a clearer view of how a facility has been functioning over time. The intent is not to “rate” facilities for you, but to surface patterns and signals that can guide your questions and decisions.

Any views shown here are built from public data sources only. They do not replace in-person visits, medical advice, or state-level guidance, and they do not accept any payments or incentives from facilities.

How Maisage thinks about facility data

Many rating systems reduce complex operations into a single score. Maisage is more interested in patterns: how staffing changes over weekends or holidays, how often EMS is called, whether harm-level citations repeat, and how ownership or management changes over time.

No single number can tell you whether a facility is “good” or “bad.” But trends and signals can help you ask sharper questions, choose where to visit, and decide what to monitor if someone you love lives there.
