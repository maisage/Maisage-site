Facility Explorer – turning public data into usable guidance

Facility Explorer is where Maisage pulls together public information about nursing homes, assisted living, and related care settings — and turns it into clearer signals for you and your family. Instead of relying only on star ratings, the goal is to show patterns: staffing stability, emergency activity, incidents, and regulatory history.

All tools here are built on publicly available data (CMS, state health agencies, court and emergency service records where available). No resident-level information or private health data is used. The purpose is transparency and understanding, not marketing or referral fees.

Future tools Facility Explorer could grow into

Over time, this area can evolve from simple views of public data into a richer set of tools:

  • Weekly safety watchlists – highlighting facilities with rising EMS calls, staffing drops, or repeated incident reports.
  • Medicaid-readiness signals – showing where Medicaid beds are more stable, and how facilities balance private pay and Medicaid residents.
  • Memory care safety indicators – focusing on elopements, supervision issues, and dementia-specific staffing patterns.
  • End-of-life care signals – for facilities working closely with hospice, tracking patterns that affect comfort and dignity at the end of life.

Even as these tools expand, the core goal stays the same: help you see how a place is really functioning, using only public information and clear explanations — no paid listings or hidden incentives.
