Services & Payments

Services to help you make sense of your options

Most of Maisage is focused on free, plain-language information — so you can understand Medicare, Medicaid, facilities, medication costs, housing, and daily living supports without pressure or sales.

These services are for when you want more personal help: someone to walk through your situation with you, organize options, verify information, and help you think through trade-offs in a calm, focused way.

All work is based on publicly available information and your goals. Nothing here is legal, financial, or medical advice — it is guidance to help you see your options more clearly so you can make informed decisions. You stay in control of all accounts, applications, and final choices.

Deeper research, applications & daily-life support (hourly)

Some situations are more complex: multiple facilities to compare, several programs to evaluate, or a mix of benefits, housing, and daily-living questions all at once. In those cases, we can work on an hourly basis with a clear estimate agreed before starting.

Programs & application support

Help understanding and preparing for complex programs — using public information and plain language. You stay in control of forms, accounts, and submissions.

Examples include:

  • Medicaid long-term care applications and eligibility basics.
  • VA benefits (including Aid & Attendance) rules and documentation needs.
  • Social Security, SSI, or SSDI forms — what questions mean and what’s needed.
  • Low-income senior housing, rent support, and local housing authority processes.
  • Utility assistance (LIHEAP, local hardship funds, water bill help).
  • Property tax relief for seniors (exemptions, freezes, deferrals where available).
  • Other aging-related applications: HCBS waivers, caregiver support programs, etc.

I never submit forms, manage accounts, or store sensitive identifiers — I help you understand what’s being asked, organize documents, and feel prepared.

Bills, statements & insurance understanding

Many older adults start to feel overwhelmed by changing bills, renewals, insurance letters, and card charges. I help make the picture clear again.

Examples include:

  • Organizing monthly and yearly bills into a simple, readable system.
  • Understanding utility, phone, internet, and insurance statements.
  • Spotting duplicate charges or services you no longer use.
  • Basic structure of car insurance and life insurance in plain language.
  • High-level Medicare and Medigap cost comparisons using public info.
  • Fraud and scam awareness for phone, mail, and online contact.

I do not manage money, hold accounts, or sell insurance or financial products. The goal is to help you see what you have and what it means so you can decide what to keep, cancel, or change.
