Services & navigation sessions

Practical help turning information into a plan

Maisage is for people who don’t just need information — they need someone to sift through it, connect the dots, and help them see realistic options. All work is based on publicly available information and your goals. You stay in control; Maisage helps you understand the landscape and trade-offs in front of you.

These services do not provide legal, tax, financial, or medical advice, and they do not recommend or sell specific products, plans, or facilities. The focus is transparency, planning, and plain-language explanation so you can make your own decisions.

How pricing works

To keep things simple and predictable, Maisage uses a mix of fixed prices and time-and-materials (T&M) for deeper work. You always know what’s included before anything starts.

  • Core navigation sessions – fixed price: a flat fee for a one-on-one session plus a short written summary of what we discussed and your options.
  • Written summaries / plans – fixed price: a clear document you can share with family (for example, a Family Aging Plan).
  • Extra research or modeling – T&M: if you want deeper, custom work beyond the core session, it is billed hourly — only after we agree on scope and a cap.

Navigation & research services

These are the kinds of work people most often ask for. In many cases, one core navigation session is enough. When you need deeper, custom work, we can agree on extra research billed hourly.

Consult – clarify your situation and options

A focused session to sort out where you are now and what realistic paths you have over the next few years — at home, in assisted living, or in facility care.

  • Identify 2–3 realistic care paths for your situation.
  • Talk through trade-offs in independence, cost, and risk.
  • Leave with a short written summary you can share with family.

Typically covered by one core navigation session.

Medicare Options & Plan Map

A one-on-one session to map how Medicare fits with your doctors, travel, income, and medications — without steering you to specific plans or companies.

  • Compare Original Medicare + Medigap + Part D with Medicare Advantage at a high level.
  • Consider how retiree coverage, VA benefits, or Medicaid change the picture.
  • Receive a simple written map of your realistic paths and key questions to ask SHIP, HR, or plan reps.

Typically one core navigation session; deeper comparisons can use hourly research with your approval.

Medication & cost research

Help understanding why medication prices vary so much and what options might exist within your situation, using only public and plan-published information.

  • Compare cash prices, insurance copays, discount programs, and mail-order.
  • Highlight where the largest cost differences appear to come from.
  • Provide a clear, plain-language comparison table for your current meds.

Often starts with a core session; complex sets of medications may require agreed hourly research.

Facility deep-dive & shortlist review

A closer look at a small set of facilities you are considering, using public inspection, complaint, staffing, and ownership data where available.

  • Create a shortlist of facilities around your target area.
  • Summarize inspection patterns, complaints, and enforcement activity.
  • Provide a list of specific questions to ask on tours or calls.

Usually one core session plus some agreed research time, depending on how many facilities you want reviewed.

Care path & budget planning

For families who want to see how different paths (stay home, move, hybrid) might play out financially over the next several years.

  • Outline 2–3 scenarios (home-first, facility-first, hybrid, Medicaid path, etc.).
  • Show, at a high level, how costs and resources may line up under each path.
  • Highlight when savings might be exhausted and what that could trigger.

Often requires one core session plus some T&M modeling, always with a pre-agreed cap.

Family Aging Plan (shareable summary)

A written summary you can print or email to family — capturing your goals, preferred paths, and key numbers in one place.

  • Outline what matters most to you (independence, location, type of care).
  • Summarize the care paths you are considering.
  • Include a short checklist of questions to revisit each year.

Typically added as a fixed-fee follow-up to a navigation session.

Focused topic research

When you need someone to dig into a specific question grounded in public information — such as a benefit program, state rule, or local service model.

  • Clarify the exact question you are trying to answer.
  • Pull together public sources in one place, in plain language.
  • Provide links back to original materials so you can verify and share.

Usually billed as T&M, with a clear scope and hour cap agreed in advance.

Ongoing navigation check-ins

For situations where you want light, periodic support instead of a one-time session — especially when health, family, or finances are changing.

  • Agree on a simple cadence (for example, every 3–6 months).
  • Review what has changed and whether your plan still fits.
  • Adjust scenarios and questions as needed over time.

Can be structured as a small recurring fee or occasional T&M, depending on your preference.
