Services – Your Care Path Support

Maisage does not sell products, earn commissions, or receive referral fees. Services are simply paid time spent researching, organizing, and explaining publicly available information so you and your family can make decisions with more clarity and less crisis.

All work is based on public sources you can independently verify. No legal, financial, or medical advice is provided.

1. Your Care Path Session

This is the core, structured session where we step back from the chaos and look at the whole picture: your current situation, your goals, and the realistic paths available over the next few years.

  • Review where you are now (living situation, support, main worries).
  • Clarify your top priorities: independence, money protection, location, family roles.
  • Walk through possible paths: staying home, assisted living, nursing home risk, hybrid options.
  • Identify key systems: Medicare, Medicaid, VA, Social Security, long-term care risk.
  • Outline next steps in plain language to share with your family.

A typical Your Care Path Session is around 60–90 minutes. Pricing is a clear flat fee for the session, with any additional project-style work discussed and agreed to in advance. Payment links (for example, through Square) are provided separately after we confirm the scope.

2. Facility research & support

When facility care becomes part of the conversation, families often feel overwhelmed by ratings, ownership changes, and conflicting stories. Maisage can help organize what is publicly knowable about facilities you’re considering.

Facility landscape review

  • Identify facilities in a given area using public databases.
  • Pull publicly available ratings and inspection summaries.
  • Note ownership changes and enforcement activity where visible.
  • Summarize patterns in plain language (not just star ratings).

Facility calling & verification

  • Call facilities to ask standardized questions you agree to ahead of time.
  • Clarify whether they currently accept new residents.
  • Ask about Medicaid beds, waitlists, and admission criteria (where appropriate).
  • Confirm basic information like levels of care, contact details, and tour process.

All questions are informational. Maisage does not negotiate contracts, provide legal advice, or recommend a specific facility.

3. Benefits & application support (information-focused)

Many programs exist, but they are scattered and hard to compare. Maisage can help you see what might apply to you, using only public information and your own stated situation.

Program landscape & basics

  • Medicare & Medicare Advantage – what they generally cover and what they don’t.
  • Medicaid basics – eligibility concepts and estate recovery from public sources.
  • VA benefits overview – public information on programs like Aid & Attendance.
  • Social Security & SSI – pointing to official explanations and tools.
  • Low-income senior housing and property tax relief programs where listed publicly.

Application preparation support

  • Help you understand what typical documentation is requested (from official sites).
  • Organize checklists for information you will gather.
  • Clarify the sequence: who you contact first, what forms are usually involved.
  • Identify official agencies or accredited helpers you can contact directly.

Maisage does not submit applications on your behalf, make eligibility decisions, or provide legal counsel. All guidance is based on public information.

4. Medication & medical cost research

Medication and device costs can vary widely. Maisage can help you understand patterns using public tools and sources so you can talk with your prescribers and pharmacies more confidently.

  • Compare publicly listed cash prices for certain medications at different pharmacies.
  • Review plan documents and formularies to understand tiers and coverage rules.
  • Identify public discount programs and transparent pricing tools.
  • Summarize differences between brand and generic options (from public sources).
  • Highlight questions to bring to your doctor or pharmacist.

Maisage does not change prescriptions, override medical advice, or handle pharmacy accounts.

5. Daily living & home support research

Many supports that keep someone at home are not “medical” at all. Maisage can help you map out what exists in your area using public information, so you can build a realistic mix of services over time.

  • In-home caregiver agencies and non-medical support options.
  • Meal delivery, grocery delivery, and community meal programs.
  • Housekeeping, laundry, and light chore services.
  • Lawn care and basic home maintenance resources.
  • Publicly listed home repair programs for seniors or low-income households.
  • Utilities assistance programs and cost-relief programs where available.

Maisage does not manage contractors, supervise work, or guarantee provider quality. All findings come from public directories and sources.

6. Safety, technology & staying connected

Technology can help with safety and connection, but it can also feel overwhelming. Maisage focuses on simple, realistic uses — not pushing gadgets.

  • Identify common tools for medication reminders and appointment alerts.
  • Outline options for basic fall-detection or safety wearables (from public info).
  • Explain simple ways to stay in touch with family (video calls, shared calendars).
  • Discuss safe, limited use of social media to stay updated without being overwhelmed.
  • Help you think through how much technology feels right for you.

Maisage does not monitor devices, store personal data, or provide emergency-response services.

7. Safe & enjoyable travel planning (information only)

Travel can still be one of the best parts of life — it just needs to be planned with health, energy, and safety in mind.

  • Research accessibility information for hotels, cruises, or tours (from public sources).
  • Outline realistic pacing: travel days, rest days, and backup plans.
  • Highlight general considerations for traveling with medications or equipment.
  • Identify questions to ask travel providers about mobility, oxygen, or assistance.

Maisage does not book travel, sell trips, or receive commissions. All work is research and explanation based on public information.

8. Money, bills & life organization (information-focused)

Keeping bills, policies, and account information organized is a big part of independence. Maisage can help you think through structure — not manage your money.

  • List and categorize recurring bills and obligations.
  • Identify which items may be candidates for simplification or consolidation.
  • Create simple checklists or overviews for you and your family.
  • Highlight public resources about fraud prevention and senior-focused scams.

Maisage does not manage accounts, hold funds, invest money, or give financial advice.

How payment works

Services are billed in a simple, transparent way:

  • A flat fee for a Your Care Path Session.
  • Optional time-and-materials billing for deeper research or ongoing support, discussed in advance.
  • No commissions, referral fees, or product markups — you are the only paying party.
  • Online payment links (for example through Square) will be provided once you confirm the scope.

Some people may also choose to make small voluntary donations (for example, as little as $1) to help support future educational content; when that option is live, it will be clearly marked as separate from paid services.
