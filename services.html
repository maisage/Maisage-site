    What these services do (and don’t) include

    These sessions are about clarity and planning, not clinical diagnosis or legal advice.

    What you can expect

    Orientation call (60 minutes):

    • Walk through your current situation and who is involved.
    • Clarify what decisions are urgent and what can wait.
    • Identify information gaps and questions to take back to your providers.

    Facility shortlist & question list:

    • Use publicly available data and your constraints to build a short, realistic list.
    • Highlight inspection, staffing, complaint, and ownership patterns to watch.
    • Provide a written list of questions to bring on tours or calls.

    Medication cost & complexity review:

    • Identify high-cost medications and potential alternatives to ask your prescribers about.
    • Flag combination risk areas to clarify with your pharmacist or prescribing team.

    Scenario planning session (6–24 months):

    • Compare “stay at home,” “move to assisted living,” or mixed approaches.
    • Outline likely care tasks, costs, and family roles in each path.
    • Capture trade-offs in a simple written summary.

    All summaries are written in plain language that you can share with your own medical, legal, and financial professionals.

    The care team you should build around you

    No single person or website can replace a proper care team. As you use Maisage and any services here, it’s important to loop in the right professionals.

    • Primary care physician (PCP) – anchor for overall care.
    • Geriatrician – for aging-related complexity and multiple conditions.
    • Neurologist – if there are dementia, stroke, or movement concerns.
    • Cardiologist – for heart disease, heart failure, or complex cardiac meds.
    • Pharmacist – for medication interactions, deprescribing discussions, and cost options.
    • Care manager or social worker – to help coordinate services and benefits.
    • Elder law / estate attorney – for POA, guardianship, and asset protection questions.
    • Financial planner / advisor – to understand long-term affordability and trade-offs.

    Maisage is meant to help you prepare for conversations with these professionals — not replace them.
