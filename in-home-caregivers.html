In-home caregivers and home support

Staying at home with the right help is a priority for many people. In-home support can range from short nursing visits to ongoing personal care to everyday services like cleaning and rides. Understanding the different types matters for both cost and expectations.

Terms and regulations vary by state. This page focuses on common categories you are likely to encounter.

Types of in-home services

  • Home health – usually short-term, medically focused care (nurses, therapists) ordered by a doctor, often covered under certain conditions by Medicare or insurance.
  • Personal care/attendant care – help with daily activities such as bathing, dressing, toileting, and meal preparation, often paid privately or through Medicaid or other programs.
  • Private-duty or agency caregivers – caregivers arranged through agencies or directly by families, with hourly rates and schedules determined case by case.

The same person may receive a mix of these over time, so clarity about what is covered and what is private pay is important.

Using ordinary services to extend independence

Not all support has to be labeled “care.” Many people use:

  • Meal delivery or local meal programs.
  • Grocery delivery and pharmacy delivery.
  • Transportation services or rides from family and friends.
  • Housekeeping or yard services.

These can reduce strain and risk without immediately moving into a long-term care setting, and they can be easier for family to share or rotate.
