About Maisage

Why Maisage exists — and who it’s for

Maisage was created to give people and families clear, grounded guidance for aging, care, and end-of-life decisions — without sales, pressure, or hidden financial interests. It’s a place to understand the full cost picture, plan ahead, and keep control of your own life for as long as possible.

The founding story

Maisage began from a deeply personal place — born out of love, exhaustion, and a quiet realization that the system meant to support aging adults often abandons them instead.

I watched my mother, my mother-in-law, and now my husband’s brother each walk their own end-of-life path. Every one of them faced a maze of paperwork, inflated costs, and missing information. Families weren’t failing their loved ones — the system was failing the families.

Over and over, I heard the same sentence — from doctors, social workers, friends, even other families trying to help:

“When the money runs out, Medicaid will take over.”

That single line has become the unspoken plan for aging in America. It’s what most families cling to when the money dwindles, because no one has shown them another way. But Medicaid, while essential, was never meant to be the only plan. It takes over when independence is already gone — when choices are no longer your own.

And the people making those choices? Too often, they barely know you. Estranged adult children, ex-spouses, or distant siblings suddenly step in after years of silence. Health-care professionals who’ve only known you for a week or two are left to decide how you’ll live the rest of your life. Decisions about your future are made by people who have never lived your life.

I built Maisage to change that pattern.

Maisage exists primarily to guide you — to help you take control of your own aging, stay independent longer, and design an ending that reflects who you are. It shows how to plan for in-home hospice care, understanding what’s covered and what isn’t, so you can stay where you’re most comfortable without financial or emotional surprises. It also helps you engage your family in ways that are manageable for them and meaningful for you, giving everyone a shared map instead of a last-minute crisis.

Maisage isn’t about managing care — it’s about managing life with foresight, grace, and self-respect.

It’s for anyone who wants to make the next 30 years as intentional and independent as the life they’ve already lived.

Let’s make the next 30 years on your terms — just as your life has been.

What Maisage focuses on

Transparency

Clear, accessible information on how care, services, and costs really work — so you and your family can see the true picture and avoid surprises.

Independence

Tools and scenarios to help you stay in control of where and how you live, for as long as possible, instead of letting default systems decide for you.

Dignity

Guidance that starts with you — your values, your comfort, your boundaries — and then helps your family align around choices that respect who you are.

How to use this site

Maisage is not a provider, agency, or broker. It doesn’t sell products or take referral fees. Instead, it gives you a structured way to understand options and plan:

  • Learn – Get plain-language explanations of Medicare, Medicaid, hospice, and medication costs.
  • Plan – Explore what it really costs to age at home, hire help, or use mixed family support.
  • See Scenarios – Compare different paths, including Medicaid spend-down and hybrid family plans.
  • Use Tools & Maps – Start translating abstract choices into concrete numbers and tradeoffs.

You stay in charge of your decisions. Maisage is here to help you see the path more clearly.
