What Maisage is, and why it exists

Maisage was created to give you clearer information, calmer options, and more control over how your later years unfold — before a crisis makes the decisions for you.

A personal beginning

Maisage began from a deeply personal place — born out of love, exhaustion, and a quiet realization that while there is a huge amount of information about aging and support out there, very little of it is clear, connected, or easy for families to use.

I watched my mother, my mother-in-law, and now my husband’s brother each walk their own end-of-life path. Every one of them faced a maze of paperwork, inflated costs, and missing information. People were trying their best, but the system was scattered, confusing, and rarely explained in plain language.

Over and over, I heard the same sentence — from doctors, social workers, friends, even other families trying to help:

“When the money runs out, Medicaid will take over.”

That single line has quietly become the “default plan” for aging in America. It’s what many families hold onto when savings start to thin out — not because it’s what they truly want, but because no one has shown them other paths in a way that feels understandable and doable.

Medicaid is an essential safety net. But it was never meant to be the only plan. It generally steps in when independence is already gone — when where you live, what support you get, and what choices you have are mostly decided by systems, not by you.

And the people making those decisions? Too often, they haven’t had much day-to-day interaction with you in years. Estranged adult children, ex-spouses, or distant siblings may suddenly step in. Health-care professionals who have only known you for a week or two are left to decide how you’ll live the rest of your life.

It’s a little like dating someone for a few weeks, then getting married and expecting a lifetime of understanding. Those decisions often fall in full crisis mode — rushed, emotional, and backed into a corner. It is not fair to you, and it’s not fair to the person who has to step in and decide under pressure.

Maisage was built to change that pattern.

Instead of waiting for the system to decide for you, Maisage helps you filter through the massive amount of information available, understand the rules and structures that shape your options, and build a care and life plan for your later years that actually reflects who you are.

It arms you with knowledge and information to enable you to make informed decisions — not just about medical care, but about where you live, how you’re supported, how your money is used, and how your family is involved. That includes, but is not limited to, hospice or end-of-life care. It also covers everyday realities like staying at home longer with support, using ordinary services wisely, and knowing when facility care may become necessary.

Maisage also recognizes that you don’t live this alone. Family and loved ones are part of the picture — but they shouldn’t only be called in at the last minute. This platform is meant to help you bring them into the conversation earlier, in a way that is manageable for them and still centered on you.

The goal is simple: fewer decisions made in the heat of a crisis, and more made with calm, clarity, and a shared understanding of what matters most to you.

Who Maisage is for

Maisage is for anyone who wants the next chapter of life to be shaped by intention, not just by default:

  • Adults planning ahead for their own later years — while they’re still independent and clear-headed.
  • Spouses or partners trying to balance care, finances, and their own well-being.
  • Adult children who want to help without taking over, and who don’t want to wait for a crisis call.
  • Families who have already had a hard experience with the system and don’t want to repeat it.

If you want to understand the landscape — Medicare, Medicaid, medications, housing, home support, facility options, and trade-offs — and turn that into a practical plan, Maisage is designed with you in mind.

The values behind Maisage

Everything on this site is built around three core values — transparency, independence, and dignity.

How to use Maisage
