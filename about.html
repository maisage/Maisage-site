About Maisage

Why Maisage exists — and who it’s for

Maisage was created to give people and families clear, grounded guidance for aging, care, and end-of-life decisions — without sales pressure, hidden incentives, or confusing jargon. Its purpose is to help you understand the full cost picture, plan ahead, and stay in control of your own life for as long as possible.

The founding story

Maisage began from a deeply personal place — born out of love, exhaustion, and a quiet realization that the system meant to support aging adults often abandons them instead.

I watched my mother, my mother-in-law, and now my husband’s brother each walk their own end-of-life path. Every one of them faced a maze of paperwork, inflated costs, and missing information. Families weren’t failing their loved ones — the system was failing the families.

Over and over, I heard the same sentence — from doctors, social workers, friends, and even other families:

“When the money runs out, Medicaid will take over.”

That single line has become the unspoken plan for aging in America. Families cling to it because no one shows them another way. Medicaid, while essential, was never meant to be the only plan — it steps in when independence is already gone.

And the people making decisions at that point? Often they barely know you — estranged family, ex-spouses, or healthcare professionals who may have known you for only days. Decisions about the rest of your life can end up made by people who were never part of your life.

I built Maisage to change that pattern.

Maisage exists to help you take control of your own aging, stay independent longer, and design an ending that reflects your values. It guides you through in-home hospice planning, understanding coverage, and engaging family in ways that are meaningful and manageable — before a crisis arrives.

Maisage isn’t about managing care — it’s about managing life with foresight, grace, and self-respect. It’s for anyone who wants to make the next chapter of life as intentional as the ones already lived.

Let’s make the next 30 years on your terms — just as your life has been.
